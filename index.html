Volvo

1927 – The first car rolls out of the factory On the morning of April 14, 1927, the first Volvo car drove out of the factory gates in Gothenburg on Sweden's west coast. The car was officially called the ÖV4 and was an open model with a four-cylinder engine. The world's first Volvo was ready to hit the roads.

Saab

In 1966, Saab launches its first four-stroke engine. In 1969, Saab-Scania AB is formed when Saab AB and Scania-Vabis AB are merged. In 1971, Saab presents two innovations; electrically heated driver's seat and self-repairing bumpers. 1976 in January, the millionth Saab car leaves the production line in Trollhättan.
